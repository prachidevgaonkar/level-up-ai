# Programme Governance & Operating Model

The Level Up AI programme runs on eight principles. They exist to protect fairness, reward genuine effort, and ensure the leaderboard reflects real adoption — not performative compliance.

---

## The Eight Programme Rules

### Rule 01 · Squad-Based Competition

Participants are organised into squads of six to ten members, drawn from across functions and seniority levels where possible. Each squad competes as a unit to accumulate the highest total XP across all six quests throughout the programme season.

**Why squads?**  
Individual competition alone creates pressure without support. Squads create accountability, mutual coaching, and a shared stake in each other's success — which is precisely the culture AI adoption requires.

**Squad formation:**
- Squads are formed by the programme team at season launch
- Members may not transfer between squads mid-season without AI Adoption Manager approval
- Squads of fewer than 4 active members may be merged at the programme team's discretion

---

### Rule 02 · Weekly Evidence Submissions

All XP claims must be supported by evidence submitted by the squad lead every Friday before 17:00 local time, using the squad tracker template.

**Acceptable evidence types:**
- Screenshots with context annotations
- Workflow documents (before/after)
- Written summaries signed off by a colleague or manager
- Certification completion certificates
- Meeting attendee lists or calendar invites

**Evidence that does not qualify:**
- Verbal reports without written backup
- Screenshots without context or dates
- Evidence older than two calendar weeks

Late submissions (received after Friday 17:00) are held for the following week's review cycle.

---

### Rule 03 · AI Adoption Managers

Each squad is assigned a dedicated AI Adoption Manager (AIAM). The AIAM is not a member of the competing squad — they are a neutral programme role responsible for:

- Reviewing and validating all XP evidence submitted by their squad
- Coaching squad members who are falling behind or disengaged
- Escalating breach allegations to the jury panel
- Representing their squad at the monthly review
- Ensuring XP allocation is fair, consistent, and evidenced

AIAMs do not earn XP and do not appear on the leaderboard. They are assessed separately on programme participation quality.

---

### Rule 04 · Monthly Jury Review

A cross-functional jury panel convenes monthly to:

1. Review and finalise all XP claims from the prior month
2. Hear and adjudicate any XP disputes or breach appeals
3. Review programme health metrics and flag risks
4. Approve any proposed rule changes or season adjustments

**Jury composition:**
- 1 × Programme Lead (chair)
- 2 × Business stakeholders (rotating)
- 1 × AI Adoption Manager (rotating)
- 1 × External advisor or sponsor (where available)

Jury decisions on XP disputes are final. Decisions are recorded in the programme log and shared with all squad leads within 3 working days.

---

### Rule 05 · Live Leaderboard, Published Weekly

Individual and squad rankings are published on the internal AI Adoption Hub every Monday morning without exception.

**The leaderboard shows:**
- Individual rank, XP total, and current mastery level
- Squad rank and total XP
- Weekly XP movement (gain/loss from prior week)
- Top 3 XP earners of the week (named, with their top activity)

**The leaderboard does not show:**
- Penalty breach details (these are communicated privately)
- Pending XP claims (only validated XP appears)
- Members who have opted out of individual visibility (squad-level only)

Members may request individual leaderboard anonymity from the programme team. Squad-level participation remains mandatory and visible.

---

### Rule 06 · Monthly Reward Ceremonies

Recognition and rewards are distributed monthly — not held until season end. This is deliberate: long feedback loops kill momentum.

**Monthly ceremony structure:**
- Held at the monthly all-hands or a dedicated programme session
- Top 3 individual XP earners named and recognised publicly
- Top squad of the month named and recognised
- One "most improved" award for the member with the highest XP jump week-on-week
- Any new rank unlocks from the prior month announced publicly

**Reward tiers by rank:**
| Rank | Reward Tier |
|---|---|
| Curious | Public mention + digital badge |
| Explorer | Leaderboard feature + badge |
| Integrator | Physical reward package (standard) |
| Innovator | Premium reward package + AI Champion title |
| Champion | Premium reward package + executive recognition |
| Sovereign | Elite reward package + Hall of Fame + client feature |

Reward specifics are set by the organisation running the programme and are not defined in this framework.

---

### Rule 07 · Wildcard Challenges

At a minimum of once per season, the programme team will announce an unscheduled wildcard event. These are time-limited bonus challenges designed to inject energy, surface unexpected champions, and reward creativity.

**Examples of wildcard events:**
- **AI Hackathon** — 48-hour challenge to redesign a named business process using AI. 2× XP multiplier on all qualifying activities.
- **Prompt Battle** — Submit your best prompt for a specific use case. Top 3 voted by the team earn bonus XP.
- **Tool Discovery Sprint** — First 5 members to log and use a specific new tool earn 50 bonus XP.
- **Cross-Squad Mentoring Week** — Mentor a member from a different squad and both earn 30 bonus XP.

Wildcards are announced with a minimum of 48 hours' notice and a maximum duration of 7 days.

---

### Rule 08 · AI Hall of Fame

At the close of each programme season, all members who reached Sovereign rank are permanently inducted into the AI Hall of Fame.

**Induction includes:**
- Permanent named entry on the AI Hall of Fame page (internal hub)
- Individual profile published in the season-close programme report
- Name and achievement shared in company-wide communications
- Name shared with key clients and stakeholders (with consent)
- Invitation to the season-close recognition event

The Hall of Fame persists across seasons. Members inducted in prior seasons retain their entry permanently.

---

## Rule Changes

Programme rules may be amended between seasons. Mid-season rule changes require:

1. A proposal submitted to the jury panel
2. A majority vote by the jury
3. At least 5 working days' notice to all participants before the change takes effect
4. Documentation in the programme log

No rule change may reduce XP already earned or remove a rank already unlocked.
