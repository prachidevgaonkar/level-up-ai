# Adoption Metrics

The programme tracks four outcome-based indicators. These metrics exist to demonstrate business value, identify where adoption is stalling, and make the case for continued investment in AI capability-building.

Activity metrics (logins, sessions, clicks) are deliberately excluded. Only outcomes count.

---

## Metric 01 · Tool Activation Rate

**Definition:** The percentage of programme participants who have used at least one approved AI tool in a real work context within the past seven days.

**Target:** 80% by end of Season 01

**Why It Matters**  
Activation rate separates participants who are aware of AI from those who are actually using it. A high activation rate indicates that the programme is driving genuine behaviour change, not just generating awareness.

**How It Is Measured**  
- Participants self-report usage via the weekly squad tracker
- Usage must be in a real work context (not a sandbox or tutorial)
- Reported by squad leads and validated by AI Adoption Managers monthly

**Reporting Cadence:** Weekly (squad level) · Monthly (programme level)

**Baseline:** Establish in Week 1 via a pre-programme survey asking participants to report current AI tool usage.

---

## Metric 02 · Time Saved Per Person Per Week

**Definition:** The average number of hours saved per programme participant per week as a direct result of AI tool use, based on participant self-reporting.

**Target:** 3 hours per person per week by end of Season 01

**Why It Matters**  
Time saved is the most direct proxy for AI ROI at the individual level. Aggregated across the team, it translates directly into capacity — the equivalent of hiring additional headcount, at the cost of a training programme.

**How It Is Measured**  
- Participants log estimated time savings in the squad tracker when submitting XP evidence
- Savings must be attributed to a specific AI tool or workflow change
- AI Adoption Managers review estimates for plausibility and flag outliers
- Aggregated monthly by the programme team

**Reporting Cadence:** Monthly (individual estimates) · Quarterly (programme aggregate)

**Calculation**
```
Total time saved = Sum of all participant time savings in the period
Average per person = Total time saved ÷ Number of active participants
Annualised value = Average per person × 48 working weeks × headcount × average hourly cost
```

**Notes**  
Time savings should be estimated conservatively. Squad leads are expected to challenge estimates that appear inflated. A peer review step is built into the XP validation process for Stage III and Stage IV workflow integration claims.

## Metric 03 · Workflows Permanently Rebuilt with AI

**Definition:** The cumulative number of recurring, business-critical workflows that have been permanently redesigned to embed AI, as evidenced by 30+ days of sustained AI-assisted operation and documented outcome.

**Target:** 15 workflows by end of Season 01

**Why It Matters**  
Workflow redesign is the highest-value adoption behaviour. A single redesigned workflow can save hundreds of hours annually and compounds in value as the team grows. This metric captures structural change, not just experimentation.

**How It Is Measured**  
- Members submit a workflow redesign claim via the Quest 02 evidence template
- Evidence must include: before/after workflow description, AI tool used, documented outcome, and 30-day usage confirmation
- Claims are reviewed and approved by the AI Adoption Manager
- Approved redesigns are logged in the Workflow Registry (maintained in this repo under `/templates/`)

**Reporting Cadence:** Monthly

**What Counts as a Workflow**  
A workflow qualifies if it is:
- Recurring (happens at least weekly or monthly)
- Business-critical (has a named owner and a defined output)
- Permanently changed (the AI-assisted version has replaced the prior method entirely)

One-off tasks, ad hoc requests, and experimental pilots do not count until they are formalised as permanent changes.

---

## Metric 04 · AI Champions (Innovator Rank and Above)

**Definition:** The number of programme participants who have reached Innovator rank (1,000 XP) or above at any point in the current season.

**Target:** 10% of participants by end of Season 01

**Why It Matters**  
AI Champions are the programme's most valuable output. Each one is a force multiplier — a practitioner who has already demonstrated the ability to drive adoption in others. Champions are the fastest, most cost-effective way to scale AI capability across a large organisation.

**How It Is Measured**  
- Rank is tracked automatically in the central XP tracker
- The number of Innovator+ participants is reported weekly in the leaderboard
- Champion profiles are published monthly in the internal newsletter

**Reporting Cadence:** Weekly (count) · Monthly (profiles and case studies)

## Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

The four programme metrics are lagging indicators — they measure outcomes after the fact. For real-time programme management, track these leading indicators weekly:

| Leading Indicator | What It Predicts |
|---|---|
| % of members who submitted XP evidence this week | Activation rate in 2–3 weeks |
| Number of Stage II (Try) XP claims this week | Integration rate in 4–6 weeks |
| Number of prompt library submissions this week | Evangelism and peer adoption in 3–4 weeks |
| Number of members with no XP in 2+ weeks | Risk of drop-off and disengagement |

Squad leads should review leading indicators every Monday alongside the leaderboard and flag at-risk members to the AI Adoption Manager.
